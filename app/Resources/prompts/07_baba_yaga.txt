-Little hut, little hut! Turn your back to the forest, and your front to me!
The poorly lubricated joints of the chicken legs creaked, dust fell from the cracks - and the hut slowly and majestically turned its façade to Ivanushka. The door swung open, and an old crooked hag in a colorful shawl appeared on the threshold.
- Oh! - was all the hag said, looking at the guest. She grabbed the door frame with one hand, and crumpled the shawl on her chest with the other. - Hello, Ivanushka...
-And hello to you too, hostess! Aren't you Baba Yaga?
- Granny... I... yeah! - the old woman swallowed and took a step back, - Well, why are you standing on the threshold? Come in, since you came. I'll feed you, give you something to drink, give you a steam bath... But don't be afraid, I won't eat you.
Ivanushka entered the hut, looked around and sat down on the bench. Baba Yaga settled down opposite, her tearful eyes glistening from under her tangled grey hair.
“I came to you on business, grandma,” Ivan began.
- Of course, it's business, - nodded Yaga, pushing the pot towards Ivan. - Eat, eat, my dear. You'll have time to tell me more.
- I'm not hungry, thank you. - Ivan sniffed. - Cabbage soup?
-They are the best. Do you love them?
- Aha! - Ivanushka took a spoon. - Well, maybe just to try... My Vasilisa made great cabbage soup...
“Really?” Baba Yaga asked politely.
-Uh-huh...-Ivanushka nodded with his mouth full, swallowed and added: - By the way, that's why I came.
-About the cabbage soup?- Yaga chuckled.
- No, about Vasilisa. Koschei stole her from me, the bastard. And where he put her - no one knows. They say you know everything in the world; maybe you know where I can look for her?
“Maybe I do,” Baba Yaga said in a wooden voice.
-Tell me!- Ivanushka leaned forward, almost knocking over the pot of cabbage soup onto the floor.
Baba Yaga lowered her head and stared at her palms, as if she had never seen anything more interesting in her life. After a minute of silence, she asked indifferently:
-Do you love her?
“More than life!” Ivanushka answered with feeling.
-And Vasilisa...what is she like?
-She is... Beautiful!- Ivanushka smiled dreamily.
-What are you saying... What if Koschei turned her into a frog?
“And did he transform it?” Ivanushka became alarmed.
-Maybe yes, maybe no. Answer when asked! - the old woman shouted.
“It doesn’t matter,” Ivanushka shook his head. “For me, she’s always Beautiful. Even if she’s a frog.”
-And how will you recognize her, the frog, among others?
“My heart will tell me!” Ivanushka answered without a shadow of a doubt.
-Heart, you say...
Baba Yaga smiled wryly - the way only old witches who have been beaten by life and seen it all can smile.
-Well then listen, my dear. I'll tell you the truth. Koschei the Deathless bewitched your Vasilisa and locked her in a tower. She lies there neither alive nor dead, as if made of stone. And no one can disenchant her. That's how it is.
-What should we do, grandma?
-What to do? There's nothing to be done about it...
-This can't be, grandma! There must be some way out!
Baba Yaga raised her eyes to Ivanushka. And then immediately looked away.
-Well, yes, there is. There is one way out. Kill Koschei and the curse will lift.
-How can I kill him? He is Immortal!
- Who knows, Immortal! His death, if you want to know, is at the end of a needle. And the needle is in an egg, the egg is in a duck, the duck is in a hare, the hare is in a chest, the chest is on an oak tree, and the oak tree is on Buyan Island.
-Where is Buyan Island?
-I don't know. Look.
-Yes? Well, thank you, grandma, for the advice. You helped me out. I think I'll go.
-Where are you going at this hour of the night? At least spend the night, morning is evening...
- Smarter, I know. No, I have no time. I'll go look for Buyan Island. The sooner I get there, the faster I'll save my Vasilisa.
-But it's night...
-What is it about night? We, heroes, are used to it. Maybe I won't get lost.
-Well, at least some pies for the road?
-Pirozhkov? That's good. Give me the pirozhki.
Baba Yaga slowly packed Ivanushka's knapsack; she put in some pies, some healing herbs, and an embroidered towel...
-If it gets really tough, throw the towel on the ground. And here's some soap and a comb. If the towel doesn't help...
-Should they be thrown to the ground too?
-Yes.
-So that the forest grows? And the mountain?
“Yes,” Yaga nodded.
- Clever! - Ivanushka nodded approvingly, hiding the soap and comb in his knapsack. - My Vasilisa was also a master at such things.
Ivanushka threw the knapsack over his shoulder, leaned over to Baba Yaga and kissed her on the forehead. The old woman froze, her eyes wide open.
- Thank you, grandma, for everything. I will remember your kindness forever!
-Take care of yourself... Ivanushka! - Baba Yaga said with difficulty. She reached out to stroke Ivanushka's cheek, but he had already turned around and walked away, into the rapidly thickening twilight.
The old woman resolutely rubbed her face with dry palms and returned to the hut.
“Well, what did you tell him?” the learned Raven rasped.
“It’s none of your business!” the witch snapped.
-What is this wild nonsense about needles in a duck? Couldn't you just tell him that Koschei is a man like everyone else? He eats rejuvenating apples every day - that's why he doesn't age. And just hit him over the head with a sword - that's all!
- How quick! - Yaga winced. - I know myself that he is not Immortal. But...
Baba Yaga sighed and began to wipe the already clean table with her apron.
-Ivanushka won't be able to handle Koshchei. Do you know how many guards there are! And Koshchei himself is also... no gift. I know that.
- What a fool! - The raven croaked contemptuously. - Don't you understand yourself - the rejuvenating apples are your only chance! If you had told Ivanushka everything as it is, do you think he wouldn't have understood? Who else will save you if not him?
- I can't! - Baba Yaga sat down on the bench and burst into tears. - Well, I can't send him to certain death! It's better to let it be... like this.
- Well, why did you send him to hell? There is no Buyan Island! And there never was. You just made it up yourself. Why are you making a fool out of Ivanushka? He believed you, now he will look for you. Couldn't you lie and say that there is no Vasilisa anymore; Koshchei ate her and spat out the slippers?
- I couldn't, - Baba Yaga sighed. - How could I deprive him of all hope? I couldn't bring myself to say it. And he wouldn't have believed me. After all, I know him...
The old woman sobbed and wiped her nose with her sleeve.
-And so... well, let him be a fool... at least he'll be alive.
