• I must warn you right away,- said the employer,- magic doesn’t work in our world!
 • What, at all?- the Princess was upset.
 • In the form you’re used to - yes.- The employer sternly twitched his mustache.- Ours is a world of advanced science and high technologies. On the other hand, the scientific approach allows us to solve problems no worse than sorcery.
 • But magic is also a science!- objected the Princess.
 • Not at all. But science can be magic. We’ve studied this question thoroughly. You do know that everything in the Universe has a triune nature?
 • What-what?- asked the Gnome.- Excuse me for interrupting, but why triune? There are only five primary elements, as far as I know.
 • Four,- interjected the Halfling.- Water, Earth, Fire, and Air.
 • Plus Metal,- explained the Gnome.- You forgot about Metal.
 • And Nature,- added the Half-elf.- And there’s also Life, Death, Order, and Chaos.
 • Guys, don’t talk about things you don’t understand,- winced the Princess.- Elements can be primary, secondary, simple, complex, derivative, and all sorts of others, and you’re lumping them all together!
 • Ahem!- the employer reminded them of himself.- So, everything has a triune nature: Matter, Energy, and Information. One influences the other, and one doesn’t exist without the others. You can convert matter into energy and vice versa, you can affect them by changing information, to turn a particle into a wave or vice versa…
 • What nonsense,- grumbled the Gnome.- Nonsense and heresy!
 • I didn’t understand anything either,- joined him the Halfling.
 • But the young lady seems to have understood me!- said the employer.- After all, that’s exactly how magic works in principle, right? What are spells if not packets of compressed information in verbal form, designed to change the properties of objects, cause energy fluctuations, initiate the transition of something from one state to another?
 • And now I don’t understand either,- admitted the Princess.- Spells are… well, they’re just spells! Magical formulas that work because… because it’s magic! The employer looked at her condescendingly.
 • But our scientists believe that to create a working verbal construct capable of transforming reality properly, a huge amount of information must be invested in each sound. For this, a special language was developed…
 • We also use the language of magic for spells,- exclaimed the Princess.
 • I had no doubt,- nodded the employer.- So, with the help of special modulations, minor gestures, eyebrow play, and the like, you can compress useful information to mind-boggling density!
 • Archiving,- pronounced the Barbarian, who had been silent until now.- Hey, why are you all looking at me like that? There’s such a smart word, and I know it, so what, I can’t?
 • Archiving, yes,- said the employer.- That makes it clearer. In general, we’ve made a big breakthrough and created, as I said, the most perfect language for controlling the universe!
 • The language of magic,- clarified the Princess.
 • The language of magic,- agreed the employer.
 • So, magic does work for you after all? The employer grimaced and looked away.
 • No. Magic doesn’t work for us. Work in this direction is ongoing, of course, but…
 • I see…
 • But on the other hand!- exclaimed the employer.- But this language found another, unexpected application! Here, listen! He took a deep breath, bulged his eyes, spread his fingers, and said something like “FFLO-S!”
 • What was that?- asked the Half-elf when nothing interesting happened.
 • That was me retelling the plot of a new novella by one of our popular writers. A detective, quite convoluted, with many characters, lyrical digressions, and references to other works by the author… authoress. And all of it, as you see, easily fit into one word “FFLO-S!”
 • Wow!- admired the Halfling.- What a time saver!
 • Indeed. And theatrical performances have become much shorter. And meaningless conversations have disappeared as a class. And imagine how this has affected journalism! Any report now lasts no longer than three seconds! It’s a pity you don’t know our language; I’m speaking to you in the usual one, otherwise I’d get by with one short whistle, and you’d understand everything.
 • HM-M?- pronounced the Princess and haughtily raised an eyebrow.
 • Oh, so you know our language?!- rejoiced the employer.- Well, of course, now that you’ve expressed yourself so clearly… I have to agree with your arguments; as you quite rightly noted, the mentioned sum is somewhat understated, but this is only the initial offer, and we can agree on some increase in remuneration… say, by 5%..?
 • TYU-U!- mockingly drawled the Halfling.
 • Well, if you think so…- the employer pondered for a second.- All right, the fee can be increased by one and a half times, plus bonuses, if the problem is solved especially quickly and effectively…
 • Ah, I get it!- beamed the Barbarian.- This reminds me of how once trolls came to our walls to devour everyone and raged before the gates. They to us: “GRMRDR! HRFR!”, and we to them: “NAH!” So we talked, and everything was clear.
 • What high style!- the employer teared up.- What a wonderful sample of troll poetry! How much depth, feeling, and expression in it! And how cruelly you criticized it! Shame on you!
 • Wait a minute!- frowned the Gnome.- So, if I say some meaningless nonsense like “VZBZKRYAK” and wink with my left eye, then…
 • That’s not nonsense at all!- interrupted him the employer.- You just stated one of the main tenets of the philosophical school of naturfingers, albeit in a somewhat simplified form. Although, on the other hand, their opponents, for example, the drankwassers, do consider this doctrine nonsense, but one can argue about that…
 • Achoo!- sneezed the Halfling and wiped his nose with his sleeve.- Oops… The employer blushed deeply.
 • And I’m not going to translate that!- he declared.- And the magistrate will never agree to such demands!
 • SHCHA!- snorted the Half-elf, looking straight into the employer’s eyes. He immediately wilted and sighed heavily.
 • You didn’t have to get personal… All right, you’ve convinced me, double payment plus bonuses, but that’s our final word!
