• Aaa, let go, you bastard! Let go, I say! Put it back where you took it! Get off me, or I’ll smack you in the ear with a last right now!
 • Hey, team! - the Barbarian joyfully yelled, waving something green and wriggling in the air. - Look what I have! I caught a leprechaun! The leprechaun, held by the scruff of the neck, twisted and cursed, trying to kick the unreachable barbarian ear.
 • I’ll complain! - he yelled. - Outrageous! A poor shoemaker is sitting there, not bothering anyone, repairing shoes - but no, everyone has to grab him and mock him! Let me go, what are you clinging to?!
 • What about the pot of gold? - the Barbarian reminded him. The leprechaun rolled his eyes.
 • Oh, listen to him! Where, where would a poor shoemaker get a pot of gold from? Why does everyone think that if you’re a leprechaun, you must be rich? Where’s the logic? If I had gold, would I be repairing shoes? Hell no! I don’t have any pot of gold and never did!
 • You’re lying, aren’t you? - the Barbarian doubted.
 • I’m lying, - the leprechaun easily agreed. - There is a pot after all. But it’s not gold in it at all. You won’t like it. The rest of the team gathered, all staring at the leprechaun with interest.
 • How scary-cute! - the Princess cooed.
 • I thought they were extinct, - the Halfling said in surprise and poked the leprechaun with his finger. - Wow, a real one.
 • Probably the last of his kind, - said the Half-elf. - They were always few in number, and lately they’ve become very rare.
 • I’m not the last at all! - the leprechaun jerked to bite the Halfling’s finger but failed. - I have a family, children… they’re waiting for daddy at home, hungry, unhappy… let me go, you monsters!
 • Yeah, let him go, really, - the Gnome said to the Barbarian. - What do you need him for? Once upon a time, maybe leprechauns were a great people, but now they’re no use at all.
 • Leprechauns were a great people? - the Princess wondered.
 • Not only were, but still are! - the leprechaun assured her. - We’re the greatest people, it’s just that right now we have… temporary difficulties. For example, I’m being held by the scruff. Isn’t your hand tired yet?
 • Not yet, - the Barbarian replied. - I’ll let you know when it is.
 • The leprechauns even had their own state. But they… - the Gnome hesitated, glanced quickly at the Princess, and finished: - …blew it.
 • Not true! - the leprechaun squealed. - It wasn’t us! It wasn’t ours! You’re lying!
 • You see, - the Gnome continued, addressing the Princess, - usually, when superior enemy forces issue an ultimatum and demand surrender, everyone goes through five stages of acceptance: denial, anger, bargaining, depression, and finally acceptance. But it can depend on national characteristics. For example, we dwarves never gave up an inch of our land, and why? Because we skipped the stages of denial and anger and went straight to bargaining, and we bargained until the enemy fell into depression.
 • And we, - added the Barbarian, - never went beyond denial at all. Enemies give us an ultimatum, and we say “yeah right” and punch them in the face, and the enemies leave empty-handed.
 • Barbarians, - the leprechaun hissed.
 • Well, with leprechauns it’s the opposite, - said the Gnome. - They first readily agree to any demands, then fall into depression realizing what they’ve done, then start bargaining and try to change the situation after the fact, and when, of course, nothing comes of it, they fall into anger and rampage, even though everyone knows you don’t wave your fists after the fight. And finally, convinced of their absolute helplessness in the face of circumstances, leprechauns move to denial. Nothing happened, we don’t know anything, and we don’t want to know, that sort of thing.
 • Poor things, - the Princess sniffled.
 • Pathetic, - the Barbarian took pity and set the leprechaun on the grass. - Run to your kids, you disgrace. The leprechaun ran a few steps away, turned around, and shook his tiny fist at the Barbarian.
 • Think you’ve won? Fool! You’ll never catch me! Just you wait, you oaf! Oh, I’ll get you then..! And, shaking his fist one last time, the leprechaun silently disappeared into the tall grass.
