On the clearing from the forest stumbled a massive orc, eyeing the heroes around the campfire with boozy goodwill.
“Oh, heeey there!” he waved his massive mitt and lurched toward the fire with a wobbly stride.
“Are we not scrapping with orcs these days?” the Dwarf whispered, just to be safe, resting his hand on his axe handle.
“Doesn’t seem so,” the Halfling hissed back. “At least, not on the books.”
The Dwarf nodded and eased up a tad, but kept his grip on the weapon.
By then, the orc had made it to the fire, flopped down with a thud, and took a whiff of the stewpot. He yanked a twisted spoon from his boot and a dented flask from his pocket, swigged a huge gulp, scooped some stew to chase it, and let out a satisfied belch.
“Hit the spot! Well, cheers to the… hic!… fest.”
The heroes swapped looks.
“What’s the occasion?” the nosy Barbarian piped up.
“High Culture Day,” the orc eagerly spilled. “Ours, y’know. Orc-style.”
“Hey, that’s worth toasting!” the Barbarian agreed, whipping out his own flask and knocking one back. His pals shot him dirty looks.
“I had no clue orcs even had high culture,” the Princess admitted naively. “I figured you just brawled.”
“And boozed!” the orc chipped in. “Folks always peg us as just fighters and drinkers. But nuh-uh! We’ve got tons more! We’ve… hic!… got literature if ya must know, science, and whatchamacallit… ballet!”
“Fascinating!” the Princess gushed. “Could you recite a bit from your lit, from memory?”
“From our epic lit!” the orc corrected, hitting the flask again. “Nah, not feelin’ it.”
“Well, at least name your top-tier wordsmiths? I’ll hunt ’em down in a library myself—sounds neat!”
The orc mulled it over, digging deep.
“Facepounder, Dorkins, and obviously, Doltbek Baldydrop! Oh, and Katz to boot.”
“Odd names for orcs…” the Princess mused. “Well, ’cept Facepounder.”
“He was christened Mearn’da’driell at birth,” the Half-elf murmured. “But the paperwork botched it. Figured it’d help him blend with orcs.”
“And Dorkins was a quarterling,” the Halfling tossed in.
“And Doltbek’s a mongrel,” the Barbarian confided. “Dad was barbarian stock—like ours, but eastern flavor. Mom was goblin-ish, I reckon.”
“Goblins? Practically orcs,” the orc brushed off. “Same vibe, just crappier.”
“But goblins’d argue that! They reckon…”
“Who gives a hoot what they reckon!” the orc snapped enviously. “Doltbek’s our primo bard! Ours, not yours, capisce?!”
“So what’d this Doltbek of yours pen?”
“Bunches!” the orc growled. “Can’t recall. But heaps more than certain hacks!”
“Till they plugged him for his verses,” the Half-elf noted.
“Are we savages?!” the orc exploded. “No plugging! We simmered him in oil and chowed down.”
“Easy there, have a swig!” the Barbarian soothed, passing his flask. The orc downed it and rambled on:
“And ya know who cooked up the repeater crossbow? Who first prismed light and jarred lightning? The spook-whistle inventor, eh? Eh?!”
“Yeah, yeah, you mentioned your science gig,” the Dwarf recalled.
“And I’ll spout it again! Our science is the edgiest around. More brainiacs and witchdocs than anybody!”
“True, you’ve got standout eggheads,” the Dwarf conceded. “Zip Flamebeard, Orin Oakguard, Bloin Pickmattock…”
“And that… Offur… Of-run… y’know, the tongue-twister!” the orc added.
“Offensingerstancenshtickler,” the Dwarf confirmed. “Aka Nine-Digited. Dubbed that after a decade in a lockdown think-tank, left with nine digits total—fingers and toes combined.”
“But he kept ‘em, didn’t he?!” the orc bellowed. “Shouldn’ta slacked off from the get-go! Orders to innovate? Hop to it, push progress! But nah, he sits pondering—what’s to ponder? Grind it out! Overthinkers don’t need digits anyway. Us orcs loathe overthinkers. We’re sharp enough!”
“So why not tackle science yerselves?” the Dwarf smirked.
“We do bigger: we set the stage for science! Those whiskery whizzes’d invent zilch without our strong-arming! Our motto: Able to grind? Grind. Can’t? Pot’s there, oil’s there.”
“And the ballet bit?” the Princess nudged, switching gears. “Who’s your primo twirler? Choreo boss? And…”
“Enough!” the orc lurched up, wobbled but held, jabbing a chunky green digit at the heroes. “I see the game—you’re mocking. No respect for our lofty culture. You despise us all! You’re… bigots! Nah, not quite… ethno-snobs! Or whatever… point is, you’re scum! I’m outta here!”
He spun and staggered off.
“Gonna fetch pals… they’ll school ya on our culture.”
Muttering darkly, the orc vanished into the thick brush.
The Halfling sighed and nudged the Dwarf with an elbow.
“Guess we’re scrapping with orcs now after all.”
