“Die, villain!” cried the Hero, raising his sword.
“Fool! It’s impossible to dest…”
The Dark Lord’s head fell onto the marble floor before he could finish speaking.
“…roy!” finished the Dark Lord, emerging from the side door. “You’re trying in vain.”
The Hero looked in bewilderment from the Dark Lord to the decapitated body and back.
“Did you resurrect?”
“Who, me? No. I don’t think anyone can do that at all.”
“Then how..?”
“You didn’t understand anything,” said the Dark Lord, seating himself on the throne. 
“Who do you think you killed?”
“You. Or not..? Was that a double?”
“I don’t keep doubles. Do you really think that ‘Dark’ is the surname and ‘Lord’ is the name? This, my dear, is the title of the position. One dies—another just like him, if not worse, immediately takes his place. The line of power doesn’t interrupt for a moment; no matter how many Lords you kill, at the head of the Dark forces there will always be a Lord. Understood, fool? Fighting against us is useless. As long as at least one bearer of evil is alive, the Dark Lord is invincible. And there are too many bearers of evil in the world even for you. You’ll get tired of killing.”
