"Is this Earth?" asked the representative of the alien delegation. "We haven't mixed anything up, the address is correct, the name matches? It's just that we received your message, but the recording quality is simply terrible!"
"Yes, this is Earth," confirmed the democratically elected Temporary Leader of Humanity. "Welcome, dear guests, to our wonderful planet!"
"We're here on business, actually. As far as we could understand from your message, you would like to join the friendly family of galactic peoples, is that correct?"
"That wouldn't be bad," said the Leader of Humanity cautiously, trying to remember if the message said anything of the sort.
"So, no mistake," the alien nodded with satisfaction. "We are the admissions committee, here to quickly admit you to our Federation of the Sentient. You will now take the UGE, and with that, all formalities can be considered..."
"We'll take what?!" the Leader interrupted.
"The Unified Galactic Exam. It's nothing complicated, you'll manage, we believe in you."
"But we didn't prepare, no one warned us! Listen, dear sir..."
"I actually identify as female."
"Dear madam. Forgive me. Could we at least familiarize ourselves with the exam questions first, read some reference books, study the material?"
"Oh, come on, I'm telling you, the questions are very simple! And the tasks are trivial!"
"So there's a practical part too?!"
"Well, how could there not be? Based on the results of the test, you will be assigned the status of a sentient race and be enrolled in the Federation. We can't just accept everyone, the applicant must prove they possess at least the minimum passing level of intelligence! That's why official criteria for sentience were established, which you must meet."
"And what are these criteria?" the Leader of Humanity asked grimly.
"There are only eighteen of them. First, a sentient being must be able to count to ten, making no more than six mistakes in three attempts. Second, a sentient being is able to find all the traffic lights in a picture. Third, it will not stick a light bulb in its mouth. Fourth, it does not lick doorknobs in the frost, or ever. Fifth..."
"Madam, are you kidding me?!" cried the Leader of Humanity.
"Mademoiselle, in that case. And no, I'm not kidding." The Federation representative sighed heavily. "Perhaps the admission rules were stricter once, I don't know. But today, even these requirements are considered excessive. If you only knew how many applicants we've had to turn down in the last galactic year alone! And how many we've expelled for non-compliance! By the way... your preliminary results aren't exactly high either. A three... out of eighteen. But you'll get the minimum passing score, I believe in you."
